What is CGPA?

The CGPA stands for Cumulative Grade Point Average and scores on a scale of 0 to 10, where 10 is the maximum achievable score and 0 is the lowest.

The CGPA scale was introduced by CBSE to simplify grading for Class 10 and 12 students. Many universities later adopted similar grading systems.

How to Convert CGPA to Percentage

Multiply your CGPA by 9.5. Example: CGPA 9.2 × 9.5 = 87.4%

Steps to Calculate

  • Add subject scores
  • Divide by total subjects
  • This gives CGPA
  • Multiply CGPA by 9.5
Formula: Percentage = CGPA × 9.5

What is GPA?

GPA stands for Grade Point Average and is commonly used by North American universities. The most common GPA scale is 4.0.

GPA (4.0) = Percentage ÷ 25

What is SGPA?

SGPA stands for Semester Grade Point Average and represents performance in a single semester.

Percentage = (SGPA × 10) − 7.5
